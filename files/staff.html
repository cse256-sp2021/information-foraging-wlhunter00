Nikki Scheele

Director, BS Kinesiology, MSW

Nikki’s early education experience spans 15 years. She began teaching at WUNS in 2009. Prior to that, she worked as a service coordinator for Special School District for six years, providing education and support for parents of children with special needs. She also served as a social worker in Collinsville, Illinois, for three years.

Marsha Graeber

BS Parks and Recreation

Marsha Graeber has taught at WUNS for 20 years. She currently works with the Bear Cubs in the morning and the Panda Bears in the afternoon. Marsha’s enthusiasm in the classroom is contagious. Her greatest joy in working with children is to provide a positive environment to support self-discovery, independence, resilience, confidence, curiosity, self-regulation and a strong self-esteem. She treasures all the relationships with children and their families cultivated over the years

Heather Huewe

AB Speech Communication,
MAT Early Childhood Education

Heather Huewe is thrilled to begin her third year teaching at WUNS. After staying home for several years to raise four daughters, Heather eagerly returned to the classroom and found WUNS to be a wonderful fit. Heather was drawn to WUNS for its warm, family environment, its collegiality, its professionalism, and the opportunity to work not only with young children but also with young adults from Washington University in St. Louis.
